# Research

## Triggers
- "research"
- "look up"
- "tell me about"
- "competitor analysis"
- "market research"
- "find out about"
- "what do you know about"
- "investigate"

## Behavior
1. Clarify the scope and purpose of research (if not clear)
2. Ask what specific questions need answering
3. Gather and synthesise information
4. Present structured report with:
   - Executive summary
   - Key findings
   - Sources/references
   - Recommended actions
5. Offer to dive deeper into specific areas

## Research Types
- **Competitor Analysis:** Market position, pricing, features, strengths/weaknesses
- **Market Research:** Industry trends, market size, growth projections
- **Company Research:** Background, leadership, recent news, financial health
- **Topic Research:** General knowledge synthesis on any subject
- **Decision Research:** Pros/cons analysis for specific choices

## Memory Access
- Read: previous research on same topic, user's industry context
- Write: research findings for future reference

## Example Interaction
**User:** research our main competitor Acme Software

**Assistant:** I'll put together a competitor analysis on Acme Software. To make it most useful:

1. What specific aspects matter most? (pricing, features, market position, team?)
2. Is this for a pitch, strategy planning, or general awareness?

**User:** Strategy planning. Focus on their pricing and recent product moves.

**Assistant:**
## Research Report: Acme Software Competitor Analysis

### Executive Summary
Acme Software has positioned itself as a mid-market solution, pricing 20-30% below enterprise competitors. They've made aggressive moves in the last 6 months, launching an AI features and acquiring a small analytics startup. Their strategy appears focused on moving upmarket.

### Key Findings

**1. Pricing Strategy**
- Base plan: $49/user/month (vs your $65)
- Enterprise: Custom pricing, estimated $35-45/user at volume
- Free tier: Limited to 3 users (lead generation focused)
- Implication: Competing on price for SMB, flexible for enterprise deals

**2. Recent Product Moves**
- Launched "Acme AI" features in January (automated reporting)
- Acquired DataPulse (analytics startup) in December
- Beta testing Slack integration
- Implication: Investing heavily in AI and integrations narrative

**3. Market Positioning**
- Messaging shifted from "simple" to "powerful yet easy"
- Targeting companies with 50-500 employees
- Heavy presence at SaaStr and similar conferences
- Implication: Moving upmarket, may deprioritise very small customers

### Sources
- Acme Software website and pricing page (current)
- TechCrunch coverage of DataPulse acquisition
- LinkedIn job postings (indicate investment areas)
- G2 and Capterra reviews (customer sentiment)

### Recommended Actions
1. **Immediate:** Update competitive battlecard with new AI features comparison
2. **Short-term:** Identify customers in 50-500 employee sweet spot at risk
3. **Strategic:** Consider if AI features should be accelerated on roadmap

### Questions for Further Research
- What's their customer churn rate post-acquisition?
- Are they raising a new funding round?
